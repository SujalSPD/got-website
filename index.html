The Series

Game of Thrones is an American fantasy drama television series created by David Benioff and D.B. Weiss for HBO. It is an adaptation of A Song of Ice and Fire, a series of fantasy novels by George R.R. Martin, the first of which is A Game of Thrones.

Set on the fictional continents of Westeros and Essos, the series follows several interweaving plot lines and a large ensemble cast. The first story arc follows a dynastic conflict among competing claimants for succession to the Iron Throne of the Seven Kingdoms, with other noble families fighting for independence from the throne.

The second covers the attempts to reclaim the throne by the exiled last scion of the realm's deposed ruling dynasty. The third follows the longstanding brotherhood charged with defending the realm against the ancient threats of the fierce peoples and legendary creatures that lie far north and an impending winter that threatens the realm.

Filmed across Northern Ireland, Malta, Croatia, Iceland, and Spain, the series premiered on HBO in the United States on April 17, 2011, and concluded on May 19, 2019, with 73 episodes broadcast over eight seasons.

Main Characters

Daenerys Targaryen

Mother of Dragons, Breaker of Chains

The last surviving member of House Targaryen, Daenerys begins as a frightened girl sold into marriage to Khal Drogo. Through tragedy and fire, she hatches three dragons and rises to become a powerful queen determined to reclaim the Iron Throne. Her journey from exile to conqueror is marked by liberation of slaves and an increasingly ruthless pursuit of power.
